GIX changelog

1.8

  • Added an automated check for any outages at our cloud provider, with a notification displaying their impact on GIX

1.7

  • Added human gene essentiality data from the DepMap

1.4

  • Added support for Escherichia coli (strain K12)
  • Added support for Salmonella Typhimurium (strain LT2)

1.3

  • Protein expression data has been added from Proteomics DB
  • WormBase IDs and C. elegans ORFs can now be autodetected

1.2

  • The currently selected species will now be shown on the panel that is displayed when a query term cannot be matched to anything in our database, and changing the species on this panel will immediately re-query the term against the new species
  • When the no-result panel opens, it can now be closed by clicking anywhere outside the panel in addition to clicking the close button on the panel itself

1.1

  • Developed a version of the extension for Firefox that can be found at the Add-on store
  • Users can prevent the extension from activating except when the control (Linux/Windows) or command (Mac) keys are pressed through the "CTRL/⌘ required" option
  • The order of information on the results panel can be set by dragging and dropping the settings on the extension popup
  • There is now a dark theme in addition to the light theme that will apply to both the information panel and the extension popup
  • Click the arrows on the interactor table to sort by gene or evidence numbers
  • We've created an online workspace where users can paste lists of genes or text of any kind to make it easy to retrieve information on genes that may be contained in excel or word documents for example. A link to the workspace can be found at the bottom of the extension popup or at gene-info.org/workspace
  • Schizosaccharomyces pombe has been added to our list of species
  • Pathology and pathway data from UniProt and Reactome respectively are now included on the information panel
  • Users will be offered links to open search results at various sites when the extension is not able to find a match to a queried term
